About Me

Technology

Growing up, I have had an interest in technology. I have always liked to play video games and use computers. My mom works as a web developer, so I have had some influence from her. During middle school, I took a few programming courses from iD Tech through Washington University. I have also taken multiple high school programming classes, including Computer Science Principles and Cybersecurity.

Boy Scouts

A big part of my life has been Boy Scouts. I have been in the program for over 10 years. Through Boy Scouts, I have done a lot of volunteering. This includes Scouting for Food, a program where Scouts pack food into boxes that are sent to food pantries; and Jefferson Barracks Good Turn, an event where Scouts place American flags on graves at Jefferson Barracks National Cemetery on Memorial Day. In 2016, I became an Eagle Scout, and for my Eagle Scout project, I built cat perches for Tenth Life Cat Rescue. In Scouts, I have also gone to many different campouts, including Colorado, Wisconsin, and South Carolina. I am currently active through my troop as Senior Patrol Leader.

Currently, I am attending Oakville High School as a senior. Through my high school, I am a part of STL CAPS, a program that helps students work on professionalism, business relations, and workplace skills. In college, I hope to get a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and possibly a minor in Cybersecurity.
